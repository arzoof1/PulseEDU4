# Demo Script — Teacher Roster (the deep dive) ⭐

**This is the centerpiece.** The promise: a teacher opens **one screen** on the
first day and sees the **whole child** — academics, behavior, attendance,
accommodations, and safety — instead of logging into six systems. Slow down
here; this is the "aha."

> behind any teacher's roster."*

## 1. The 30-second pitch

Every teacher gets a living roster of their students. At a glance: **FAST
scores** and the **learning-gain green-check**, **ESE / 504 / ELL** flags,
**safety-plan** indicators, and a **PBIS "invisible student"** flag — each row a
complete picture. Click a name and you're in the full **student profile** with a
**multi-year FAST history**. *"This is what 'know your students' actually looks
like on day one."*

- Have the **student profile** ready to drill into.

## 3. The live demo — step-by-step

**Scene 1 — The whole class on one screen.** Open the roster. *"Twelve-plus
students, and for each one I can already see how they're doing academically,
behaviorally, and what supports they have — before I've met them."*

**Scene 2 — Accommodations & supports (point at real rows).**
- **Lila Dawson** — green **ESE** chip. *"I know on day one she has an IEP and
  what her accommodations are."*
- **Willow James** — **504** chip.
- **Nathan Brooks** — **ELL** chip. *"Language supports, surfaced automatically."*
*(IDs shown are the **local SIS ID** — e.g. Lila 487386 — never the state FLEID.
We never display the FLEID to anyone.)*

**Scene 3 — Academics & the learning-gain green-check.** Point to the **FAST**
columns (PM1 → PM2 → PM3) and the **green-check ▲** on students who made a
**learning gain** — including **within-level** moves (e.g. 1.2 → 1.3). *"This is
the same growth signal the state cares about, and it credits real progress, not
just crossing a line."*

**Scene 4 — Safety at a glance.** Show the **safety-plan** indicator on any
flagged student. *"If a child has an active safety plan, I see the shield right
here — I'm never caught unaware."*

**Scene 5 — The invisible-student flag.** Point to the **PBIS invisible** flag.
*"Same signal as the PBIS Hub's Needs-Attention list — they share one source of
truth, so they always agree. The roster nudges me to recognize the quiet kid."*

**Scene 6 — Drill into the whole child.** Click a name → the **student profile**.
Show the **multi-year FAST history** chip (trajectory across years). *"From a
roster row to a full longitudinal picture in one click."*

**Scene 7 — Group Insights / Class Composer (optional).** Show the **Group
Insights** tab / read-only **Class Composer** that proposes intensive-group
sections from FAST item-level weakness. *"It suggests groupings from the data —
read-only; your SIS stays the source of truth."*

## 4. Why it's "not quite doing it" until you do THIS

Don't list columns — tell the **story of one student**. Pick **Lila Dawson**:
*"ESE, here's her FAST trajectory, she made a within-level gain this window, no
safety plan, and she's getting recognized in PBIS. I know this child — and I
knew her before she walked in."* The wow isn't the data; it's **one screen
replacing the morning of digging.**

## 5. Talking points & objection handling

- **Sales:** onboard a new teacher's situational awareness in minutes; supports
  MTSS, equity, and safety from a single surface.
- **Training:** the green-check and invisible flag use the **same logic** as
  Insights and the PBIS Hub — surfaces never disagree.
- *"We have a SIS for this."* The SIS stores it; the roster **synthesizes** it
  into one actionable view (and Class Composer never writes back to your SIS).

## 6. Weekly HeartBEAT tie-in

What the teacher sees here, the **family** sees a parent-friendly slice of in the
**weekly HeartBEAT** — PBIS, attendance, accommodations, staff notes. *"Teacher
and parent finally looking at the same child, the same week."*
