## What this is

**Instructional Coverage** answers: *"Which standards have I (or this
team) actually delivered instruction on, vs. which are still
unlocked?"* It's the bridge between your scope-and-sequence and what
actually got taught.

It pulls from logged lesson tags, FAST item exposure, and (if enabled)
gradebook standard alignments.

## Top-of-page picker

- **Scope**: My class • Grade level • Whole school • District
  (visibility depends on your role).
- **Subject**: ELA / Math / Science / Social Studies.
- **Window**: This 9 weeks / Year-to-date / Custom range.

## Reading the grid

- **Rows = benchmark codes** in scope-and-sequence order.
- **Columns = weeks** in the window.
- Each cell is colored by coverage type:
  - **Solid blue** — directly taught (logged)
  - **Light blue** — spiraled / reviewed
  - **Hatched** — assessed but not formally taught
  - **Empty** — no coverage yet
- **Right-edge column** shows current class % mastery (linked to
  FAST item data if available).

## Sidebar summary

- **Coverage %**: standards-in-scope that have at least one solid-blue
  cell.
- **Gaps**: standards still entirely empty as of this week. Click to
  jump to that row.
- **Over-taught**: standards with 4+ weeks of coverage and < 50%
  mastery — flag for re-approach, not more time.

## Common uses

- **Pacing check**: every two weeks, pull this up at PLC and see
  whether you're tracking the scope-and-sequence.
- **MTSS justification**: "this standard has been taught 4 times
  with mastery still at 38%" is exactly what a Tier 2 referral needs.
- **End-of-year audit**: print the full year's grid as evidence for
  district reporting.

## Logging coverage

Two ways to feed this report:
- **Quick-log button** on each lesson plan or PBIS lesson entry.
- **Auto-pull** from your gradebook standards if your admin has wired
  up the integration.
